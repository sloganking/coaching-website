The Work

What We Actually Do

This isn't talk therapy. It's identity transformation backed by real-world action.

🔍 Uncover Your Limiting Beliefs

I'll ask you what you'd want if you had a genie's lamp - no limits. Most clients can't even answer at first because they're so stuck in "I can't have that." We find those patterns and break them.

🧠 Identity Precedes Action

You don't take action because you see yourself as unattractive or unlovable. Change the identity, and the actions follow naturally. I help you see through your own delusions about who you are.

After events, you bring me what happened - hookup or not, rejection or connection, what you felt. I help you catch the stories ("she left early, so I'm unlovable") before they stick. Each time, it hurts less.

Over a year of parties, workshops, and eventually week-long retreats, you'll build so much evidence that the old stories lose their power. That's how it worked for me. That's how it'll work for you.

About

Logan King

A few years ago, I was barely surviving. I grew up in a household where love was conditional on performance, where mistakes meant beatings with a belt. I learned early that the world wasn't safe, that I wasn't safe, and I spent my entire life trying to be perfect, suppressing my needs, my anger, my desires. I became a chronic people-pleaser, terrified of taking risks, convinced that suffering was the only path to anything worthwhile.

By my mid-twenties, I'd been in a seven-year dry spell. No romance, no intimacy, no touch. I'd dropped out of college and had no clear career path. I felt completely stuck, disconnected from myself, from others, and from any sense of possibility. I didn't feel like I could talk to anyone about what I was going through. I felt profoundly alone.

Then I hit rock bottom. After trying to process old emotional wounds, everything came crashing down at once. Childhood trauma, relationship trauma, years of sexual frustration and loneliness, it all surfaced. I started having panic attacks that lasted eight hours. Every. Other. Day. The pain was indescribable. I became borderline suicidal and homicidal, overwhelmed with intrusive thoughts I'd never experienced before. I was terrified of myself.

I genuinely believed I was the most hopeless case I'd ever seen.

I went to therapy, five hours a week for over a year. It helped, but it wasn't enough. I knew that if things didn't change radically, I wasn't going to survive much longer. So I made a decision: I would try absolutely everything. No more fear. No more hiding. No more playing it safe.

That's when everything shifted.

A woman I met online invited me to a BDSM party. The old me would have been too terrified to go. But I was done with the old me. I went. And that night, surrounded by people who celebrated desire, connection, and pleasure without shame, I experienced something I'd never felt before: acceptance. I connected with person after person throughout the evening - kissing, playing, being desired. The party turned into a play party (a party where sex is possible), and I experienced the kind of emotional and sexual connection I'd spent my entire life believing was impossible for me. It was the best day of my life.

The next day, I did my first MDMA therapy session. I was held and supported lovingly by my parents. I spent eight hours processing trauma, expressing everything I'd been holding back for decades, and feeling genuinely loved and supported for the first time. Another best day of my life.

Those back-to-back experiences, the BDSM party, the therapy, discovering conscious community, became what I call my rebirth. For the first time in my life, I saw a world I actually wanted to participate in. For the first time in years, I wanted to live.

Over the next year, I committed to transformation. I went to play parties as exposure therapy. I faced my deepest fears: rejection, inadequacy, being "the guy on the outside." I cried at a play party when I couldn't find connection. It was humiliating. And it was necessary. Each experience taught me something. Each risk built evidence that I could create the life I wanted.

I moved to California. I joined training programs, Somatica Institute for sex and intimacy coaching, apprenticeship with legendary coach Laurie Handlers. I immersed myself in conscious community. I learned that transformation isn't just possible, it's a repeatable process.

Today, I'm living a life I couldn't have even imagined two years ago.

I've gone from a 7-year dry spell to having five times as many meaningful sexual and intimate connections in the past two years as in my entire life before. I go to play parties and feel completely at ease. The trauma, the triggers around rejection and self-worth, they don't show up anymore. I attend touch-positive workshops and sacred sexuality retreats, and I consistently connect - sexually, intimately, playfully. A friend once asked me, half-joking, "How do you always end up cuddling with someone new at these things, and you're both smiling and laughing?" It's not magic. It's the result of doing the work.

I've been to week-long retreats focused on BDSM, Tantra, and sacred sexuality. Places where I've had experiences I never thought possible. At the last festival I attended, I was receiving a lot of compliments from both women and men. I was openly and playfully loving on a woman I liked, and she loved it. Others were playfully jealous. But here's what matters most: my heart feels whole.

I love myself now. I feel like I bring value, joy and safety to the people I interact with, especially women. The intrusive thoughts, the suicidal ideation, the homicidal rage, the resentment, all of that is gone. I'm part of a community that celebrates pleasure and connection. I feel deeply loved and cared for. I have levels of abundance in touch, intimacy, and connection that I literally couldn't conceive of before.

This isn't just "better than before." This is a life beyond what I thought was possible for anyone, let alone for me.

One day at a Tantra class in San Francisco, I met someone who reminded me of my past self, stuck, starved for connection, convinced it was impossible. And I realized: everything I'd learned could help them. Everything I'd survived had prepared me to guide others through the same journey.

That's when I knew this was my calling.

I'm not just a coach who read the books and got certified. I'm someone who lived in hell and found my way out. I know what it's like to feel completely hopeless. I know what it's like to believe you're the exception, the one person for whom it won't work.

And I'm living proof that you're wrong about that.

If you're reading this and you see yourself in any part of my story, if you feel stuck, hopeless, convinced that transformation might work for others but not for you, I'm talking directly to you. Not to some idealized version of you. Not to who you think you should be. To exactly who you are right now.

I promise: it's possible.
